## Why This Exists

itch.io thrives in the browser, but downloading dozens of indie builds through tabs gets messy fast. **itch.io App** exists so purchases, bundles, and free claims on your own account land in a proper desktop library with install paths, updates, and one-click launch.

**itch.io App** solves **one specific problem**: treating owned itch.io items like a real local collection instead of a pile of ZIP files on the desktop. It is not a replacement for discovering new creators on the site—it is the home for what you already added to your library.

If you collect jams, charity bundles, and niche tools and want them installed cleanly, **itch.io App** is for you. Creators still upload builds on the website; you still pay or claim with your own itch.io account; **itch.io App** simply turns that library into something you can launch without digging through a Downloads folder every weekend.

## Understanding itch.io App Core Components

**itch.io App** is not a new marketplace. It combines several practical pieces around your existing itch.io library:

- **Account sync** — Lists games and tools attached to your signed-in itch.io identity inside **itch.io App**.
- **Download pipeline** — Fetches the builds creators uploaded for your platform.
- **Install manager** — Tracks folders, versions, and repair/reinstall actions in **itch.io App**.
- **Library UI** — Filters owned content by status, type, or creator without leaving the desktop.
- **Update checker** — Notices when a creator publishes a newer build you are entitled to.
- **Launch layer** — Starts executables or scripts with the working directory **itch.io App** expects.
- **Local cache awareness** — Helps you free space by uninstalling titles while keeping ownership on the site.

All these work together in **itch.io App**. Buying and claiming still happen on itch.io with your own account.
